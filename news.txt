World News Headlines - June 18, 2026

1. US-Iran Peace Agreement & Oil Markets
- The United States and Iran have signed a major 14-point memorandum of understanding (MoU) aimed at ending the conflict in West Asia. Under this deal, Iran has agreed to dilute its enriched uranium in exchange for significant economic relief. The agreement also includes provisions for reopening the Strait of Hormuz.
- Following the announcement of this interim agreement, global oil prices saw a decline in early trading on Thursday, June 18.

2. Middle East Tensions
- Despite the peace agreement, Israel has reportedly continued military strikes in Lebanon, leading to warnings of a "harsh response" from Iran.

3. G7 Summit and Diplomatic Talks
- At the G7 summit, world leaders discussed various global challenges, including security risks associated with artificial intelligence and social media.
- Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi met with US President Donald Trump during the summit. Modi highlighted issues regarding the safety of Indian seafarers in the Strait of Hormuz.

4. Indian National & Security News
- The Indian Air Force conducted over 200 sorties across India to ensure the secure and timely transport of NEET-UG examination papers. A parliamentary panel recommended that the National Testing Agency (NTA) use its surplus of ₹448 crore to strengthen the examination system.
- Delhi Police dismantled an alleged Pakistan-backed terror syndicate, arresting seven individuals.
- In Maharashtra politics, speculation continues over tensions within the Shiv Sena (UBT), following assertions by leader Sanjay Raut concerning offers made to MPs.

5. Sports & Technology
- In football, Lionel Messi scored a hat-trick for Argentina against Algeria, bringing his total World Cup goals to 16, which ties the all-time record held by Miroslav Klose.
- The National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) has integrated an AI-powered predictive maintenance system to detect road damage early and improve highway safety.
